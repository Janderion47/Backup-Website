Critique of Modern Social Media Platforms

Consider, for a moment, the significance of a politician promising benefits to specific groups of supporters, it would not be unreasonabel to assume that this politican would be elected into office if they promised the most benefits to a majority of their population. If a politician was truly the best option for the governemnt position, they would not need to promise any benefits to become elected, but this benefit-promising politician is using whatever needs of the people to sway them to their side. A parallel could be drawn between this and our social media platforms, of which many have started as innocent platforms for conversing with one another, but it appears that thay are now being used to sway the masses of our population.

The fact that democratically elected politicians have influence over the people that would elect them does not seem to be a good element of modern representational democracies. Had we not used this form of democracy, we would likely be better engaged in debates with our fellow citizens. Similarly, the existence of a body of people that would have choice in determining what people would be subject to viewing, would have great affect over others; this could be compared to those that control social media platforms, and use them to influence the content they see.
