Questioning the Philosophy of William Godwin

Would Godwin support the idea of “Survival of the Fittest” and the branching-off idea that only the strongest of a group should live? Well, it does not seem very clear if Godwin would or would not, but lets explore the topic as to see any place where we could insert Godwin’s utilitariam philosophy into it.

Context

Survival of the Fittest (SotF) originally refered to Charles Darwin’s discovery that only the best fitted of a group would survive in an enviroment, those of the species who have the most favorable traits in the enviroment, would have a higher chance of survival than their peers who do not. William Godwin believed in working towards a society of which all people would be capable of thinking for themselves, critiquing all things, and generally improving and progressing our society.

I think, to better fit the philosophy of Godwin, a better question to ask would be: would it make the general happiness of the world greater if we permitted those who have the least favorable traits to perish? Would it contribute to the progression of our society, to create a policy of picking and choosing the best fitting people to live, and let the others die, as the ancient Spartans once did; permitting the elders of the city to give sight to newborns, in order to judge if they were fit, if they would survive; those that were deemed unworthy would be banished to die in the local mountains, while the healthy ones would grow up, the boys would train to be warriors, and the girls to learn the way of the household (though I personally am not sure if the girls were subject to the selection process that I am sure the boys went through). This policy was enacted by a group of people, hundreds of years ago, who were focused on their own warrior culture, focused on choosing the best, the physically fittest of the lot as to benefit their armies.

Another example, the purging of those believed to be unable to contribute to society in the country of Germany under the administration of Adolf Hitler, as they believed that if you were unable to contribute to society, then you do not deserve anything, which I observed a relatively recently made television series which explored an alternate timeline of which the Axis Powers won the second world war, and much of the allied powers were violently disrupted, and divided amongst the Axis Powers; in the show, it was required that all doctors report to the government about the health of their patients, and a doctor told a prominent military general that his son seemed to have a medical condition that would infringe on his status as a useful citizen, even offering the general some sort of poisonous syringe, commenting something around the lines of “If you don’t do it general, then they will, and we both know that we don’t want that,”. The Nazis were known for their utilitarian ideas, 100% usage of their populace, 0% unemployment rate, which was achieved by the powerful central authority that was Hitler, which included the purging of many in the holocaust, which was murder nonetheless of those believed to be lesser than pure-blooded germans. I would not disagree with the idea that many Germans under the Nazi regime believed that their racial purging in a belief that their blood made them superior was a stop forward for their people in progressing to better the German Empire.

SotF exists in the natural world, only the best animals will survive and create offspring who would most likely have the same favourable traits. On that note, I believe that Godwin would only agree with this idea of physical SotF if it contributes to his idea of improving or progressing our society.
