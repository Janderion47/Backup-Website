David Hume's created his ideas against the existence of the self, before the existence, and even the creation of the idea of the computer. Hume's texts are difficult to comprehend at times, but an analogy between Hume's ideas and modern computers can be created, to illustrate his ideas in modern vernacular. In thinking about this, Hume's ideas are essentially saying that we are computers, biological bags that respond to both external and internal stimuli. What is the significance of the fact that Hume is essentially claiming that all people are computers?
