I wonder about the significance of the concept of love, the idea that we would have deep care for another entity. I would like totalk specifically on the idea of romantic, perhaps sexual love, between one human being to another, as it seems to be one of the most important parts of our human experience. I’ve been thinking about the idea of love and how it would be connected to being human, and one question that has sat in my mind is “when did humans begin to love?”, well I think a more elaborate question is “what are the necessary and sufficient conditions of being able to love?”

Is the ability to discern between one’s self and others be necessary to be able to love? I think that the ability to discern between self and other is necessary to say “I love you” as that statement would be in recognition of one’s self and another, so I would say that it would be necessary that one would be able to discern between self and other, but this example also requires that verbal language is also necessary. I think one other example that would assist in understanding is a person and their pet dog. Lets assume that this person greatly loves and adores their dog, but does the dog reciprocate the same feeling? I know this diverges from the idea of romantic love, but I think that in this case, the concept of love fits in any way.

How can we question if a dog is able to love if we have not established a definition of love? If one is to ask “What is love?” in public, they would most likely be replied to with the lyrics of the 1993 song “What is Love” by the band Haddaway. I think a commonly accepted definition of love or being in love with another would be that one who loves another would take extreme care of the other, and would value the other above theirself; resulting in valueing the other’s enjoyment above their own, or perhaps the one sacrificing their own life for the other.
