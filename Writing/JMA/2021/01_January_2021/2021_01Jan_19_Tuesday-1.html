Has anyone ever considered what “love” is? I know that romantic love is said to be the feeling of desire to be with another, and perhaps have some physical intimacy between yourself and the other. I simply think that the concept of love is our conscious mind trying to interpret the biological urges to seek out a mate and create offspring. I mean, why else would we be motivated by something outside of our conscious power, to seek to be with another person of which we mate for life as we want to ensure that our mate does not go to another competitor? We even recognize physical and intellectual characteristics of which we like and do not like about other people.

If it is like this, then what is the meaning of “I love (in the romantic sense) you.”? It would seem to say that in fulfilling the natural urge to mate and create offspring, you have decided that the recipient of the message has been selected because they have favorable traits which you like and you are willing to work with them as to create offspring.


It feels strange to analyze this topic in this light, as it feels to degrade some integral part of our human society. It is like sandpaper, scraping at the romanticisms in our culture, such as of men serenading the fair lady upon her balcony, though I suppose, this idea of love that the previous few paragraphs has always been known to all.

Wait, then what is the significance of people who do choose a mate, yet wish to not procreate? I feel that this brings me back to my previous thoughts of following a “natural” order, and that those who do not follow the “natural” order are bad. By definition of survival of the fittest, the desire to not procreate would then be an unfavorable trait as you would then not be procreating, ending your lineage.


I have this somewhat intrusive thought in my mind now, but would a person who is perfect in the use of logic, and is also biologically capable of procreating, decide that they do not wish to have children? This question is essentially asking what would be the logical thing to do. I think once before, I have approached the natural order idea with the idea that our ability to do logic and have a consciousness is to support our lives, and let us think in more complicated terms of how to fulfill our most basic needs of survival and our general preservation of life.
