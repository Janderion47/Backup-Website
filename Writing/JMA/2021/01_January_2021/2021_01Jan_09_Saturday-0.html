It is the morning, I’m sitting in the patio; first temperature read this morning said it was fifty degrees celsius, with a high of fifty-nine degrees celsius. My feet are cold, and I’m wearing socks and slippers right now. But at least I have the ‘Breadstick”. Oh, and on that note,

So I finally opened the ‘Breadstick’ on Thursday the seventh, and I had to put the bridge up and tune the strings, which I found wasn’t too hard, until I had to do the high ‘e’ string, which posed an issue. The other three strings were affixed to the tailpiece of the Breadstick in a loop, while the ‘e’ string was not, the end of the string was knotted in such a manner that it was sufficiently fat enough to hold it’s place on the tail piece. I believed this fixture to be sufficiently strong enough, but while I was attempting to tune the string, the balled end of the string popped off. I then messaged the luthier in a sort of panicked state of anxiety, telling them about the state of the instrument and how I might fix it. We eventually concluded that rather than balling the end again, I should tie/loop it as the other three strings are. This proved to work good, as the ‘e’ string held while I tuned it, but I have found that the there isn't sufficient amount of friction between the ‘e’ string tuning peg and peg box to hold it at proper tuning, so I have decided to tune the instrument to A4=415hz, also known as baroque pitch.

I’ve bought a fiberglass bow from the Winter Haven Carlton Music in downtown, which was the cheapest option at around forty-five dollars, while the wood bows are around ninety to ninety-nine dollars, and some special color laced carbon-fiber bows at around one-hundred-forty dollars. I also got some rosin, of which funny enough, the strings guy, Michael, suggested some different kinds of rosin to me, also stating that “it really depends on your amount of experience,” of which I told him that I have absolutely no experience with the violin, of which he then paused for a moment, and then replied “Yeah, I’ll give you this one,” which I think was one of the funniest things of my day.
