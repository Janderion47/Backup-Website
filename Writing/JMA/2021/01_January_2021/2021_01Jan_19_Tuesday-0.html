I haven’t really had the chance to write it down until now, but last Sunday, in the afternoon, my family and I went to Lake Kissimmee State Park. Its not the most eventful place, but I will say that it is very calm there. We’ve seen many animals, notably some deer and an armadillo.

There is something really nice about the quietness of the park there, its a great place to be alone as you could simply park out in their camp ground and just be alone. I think it would be a great place to bring out some friends, talk, engage with each other as you’d be forced to do so, as the cellular signal over there is very rubbish. I mentioned it in english this morning when my english teacher, Mr. Johnston, asked me how my weekend was; he mentioned that Lake Kissimmee is one of the places in Florida in which you can see the milky way, which is something that I will do in the future.

This reminds me of that one book we read in the beginning of English this school year, “Into the Wild” which most heavily focused on embracing nature and begin a part of it all, and feeling it out. With that in mind, it feels to be a great place to relax and chill and center one’s self.


When I get the chance to, when I’m older and I can be alone by myself wit h my parents’ permission, I think I’d like to spend a day alone at the park. Perhaps on another day, I would go with some friends, and we could just chill all together. I think I’ll add this to my bucket list with my friends.
