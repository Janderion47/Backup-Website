One of the most important problems that I have been considering is how an artificial intelligence should consider what to do if faced with a situation where the only options include some degree of death of some people, for example, where the artificial intelligence has to choose between crashing and killing an elderly person, or killing all passengers a car, lets say, two parents and their two young children.

We could look at the sanctity of human life argument, which would tell us to save as much life as we can, which would lead use to crash into the single elderly person. Or the benefits argument, where crashing into the elderly person would end their life, but the family would keep theirs, whereas we could have the death of the family, and the elderly person could keep theirs. We would want to have the greatest net-benefits, and would argue that we should crash into the elderly person. With these ideas in mind, we would conclude that the AI should permit the death of the elderly person.

But consider, what if we are faced with the situation where we have to choose between the death of one of two people where both of the people are pretty similar?
