Are assumptions beliefs or knowledge? Colloquially, when we make an assumption, we make a guess based on the partial knowledge of a topic that we do have; we are filling in a hole in our knowledge. We fill in these holes so that we can use the knowledge to consider more ideas that are built off of the knowledge that we filled in. Assumptions are beliefs as they are claims of knowledge that we do not have direct grounds to support, but we have interpolated/extrapolated based on the information that we do have about the topic.

When I posed this original question to the ToK class last Monday, I think I had ‘suppositions’ in mind, what I meant to ask was are suppositions beliefs or knowledge? When I think of creating suppositions, I think of the creation of hypothetical potential situations. I do not think that they are beliefs as we are not making claims of knowledge, but it cannot also be knowledge since we have not made any claims of knowledge. Within the context of the hypothetical situation, I think the suppositions would be knowledge within the situation as we have constructed a situation where the suppositions are true. What about the logic and conclusions that stem from the given information in this hypothetical situation, are they knowledge or belief? Given that we have not created any new suppositions within this hypothetical situation, within the hypothetical, I think that the logic and conclusions created are knowledge as we have solid grounds inside of the hypothetical, but outside of the hypothetical, they are simply claims of knowledge that would be completely true if the suppositions were no longer hypothetical but rather literal and true.
