Should we treat a sibling differently to comparison to an acquaintance? For simplicity, lets say that this acquaintance is certainly a person that you know but not necessarily are very close to, or you simply know as just a peer/college.

I think that we should treat bout our siblings and our acquaintances the same, because I think as a baseline, we should not treat anyone with negativity, and attempt to be as positive and appropriate as possible to all people, which I suppose would be a categorical imperative; it would be morally wrong to be openly angry toward a person where it is not appropriate. I would attempt to treat any person in this manner, as I would approve of the same manner from another person to myself. Considering the benefits argument, being negative to this person of which you are not very close to would not have any benefits and if anything, would be a detriment to the relationship between yourself and the acquaintance.

Should the fact that your sibling is more than a person that you do not know affect your treatment of them? As we have already established, I would treat them in as much positivity as appropriately as possible, so could we be any more positive/friendly? I do not think we can without discussing specific actions toward people. Considering that there exists some groups of siblings who do not have amazing relationships with each other, I think that the fact that you are siblings to each other affects how you should treat each other; I think whether you should treat a person more than the minimum of the previous categorical imperative is dependent on how much more than you are just acquaintances with the other person.


I suppose that blood relation would not necessitate/force any two people to be friends, it would then be that the two people just so happen to be related by blood in this view I suppose.



One thing that my philosophy teacher suggested the other day, perhaps in a slightly humorous manner, was that perhaps we could construct a large spreadsheet, of which I assume that one axis are full of situations of which person ‘a’ has wronged yourself, while the other axis is the specification of person ‘a’; this spreadsheet would suggest that there would be differing treatments of wrongdoers depending on who is the wrongdoer. Considering the requirement of consistency, I feel that it would be morally wrong to not be consistent within the treatment of a person based on who they are. Like, we would not change a criminal’s punishment based on race.

I do not think that we should change the way we treat a person for their wrongdoings. Sure, we would make exceptions for cases of which the decision to commit the wrongdoing was outside of the power of person ‘a’, or if it is a child who has yet to understand moral treatment of fellow people, but otherwise, our treatment of others and their wrongdoings should be consistent.
